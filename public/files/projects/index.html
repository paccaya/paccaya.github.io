Neodharma

Neodharma is committed to anti-oppression work and radical activism in a Buddhist context (though more generally a marriage between ancient wisdom traditions and contemporary philosophy). Our arrow is set upon decolonizing and diversifying biomedical sciences through medical anthropology by transitioning from an approach of pathology to one of a more universal spirit.


We also would like to explore our anthropocentric worldview and decomission our current approach of both the Industrial Growth Society and our 'return to Nature' in relation to climate change.


To properly beg the questions:

  • What does it really mean to be humans in this ever-changing, anxiety inducing present with our ephemeral past and imagined future?
  • What does it mean to be fearless?
  • What truly is freedom and how does one acquire such understanding?
  • How does the irrational compliment rationality?

Neodharma lies at the heart of these values:

To give a proper response to our polemic, polariizng political situation and what could be the Sixth Mass Extinction.
Healing a world filled with oceans of pain by being the wave of compassion in a sea of opportunities.

So how does one join this movement?

The funny thing is that you are already a part of Neodharma: one could even say that you are, in fact, the whole of Neodharma. Membership for Neodharma is not only universal but does not oppose any force, group, ideology, idea, denomination or creation. One of our most nefarious crimes on Earth is the idea of separate communities, states, and nations since all these divisions promote tribalistic warfare. We are all inclusive to the point that inclusion does not imply any exclusion, as the stratification, isolation, and division of language, groups, and identifiers are the fundamental delusion that we must overcome if we ever want to combat our meta-crises.

As Jiddu Krishnamurti once roared:

So one asks, why don't we see? Why don't we see that one of the causes of war is nationalities? Why don't we see that one may be called a Muslim, and another a Christian—why do we fight over names, over propaganda? Do we see it, or just memorize or think about it? You understand, sir, that your consciousness is the rest of mankind. Mankind, like you and others, goes through every form of difficulty, pain, travail, anxiety, loneliness, depression, sorrow, pleasure—every human being goes through this—every human being all over the world. So our consciousness, our being, is the entire humanity. This is so. How unwilling are we to accept such a simple fact, because we are so accustomed to individuality—I, me first. But if you see that your consciousness is shared by all other human beings living on this marvelous earth, then your whole way of living changes. Argument, persuasion, pressure, propaganda, are all terribly useless because it is you who must see this this for yourself.
