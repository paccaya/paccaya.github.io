Neodharma is committed to anti-oppression work and radical activism in a Buddhist context (though more generally a marriage between ancient wisdom traditions and contemporary philosophy, psychoanalysis, and existentialism). Our arrow is set upon decolonizing and diversifying biomedical sciences through medical anthropology by transitioning from an approach of pathology to one of a more universal spirit.


We also would like to explore our anthropocentric worldview and decomission our current approach of both the Industrial Growth Society and our 'return to Nature' in relation to climate change.


To properly beg the questions:

  • What does it really mean to be humans in this ever-changing, anxiety inducing present with our ephemeral past and imagined future?
  • What does it mean to be fearless?
  • What truly is freedom and how does one acquire such a feeling?
  • How does going beyond rationality compliment rationality?

Neodharma lies at the heart of these values:

To give a proper response to our polemic, polariizng political situation and what could be the Sixth Mass Extinction.

Healing a world filled with oceans of pain by being the wave of compassion in a sea of opportunities.

So how does one join this movement?

The funny thing is that you are already a part of Neodharma: one could even say that you are, in fact, the whole of Neodharma. Membership for Neodharma is not only universal but does not oppose any force, group, ideology, idea, denomination or creation. One of our most nefarious crimes on Earth is the idea of separate communities, states, and nations since all these apparent divisions promote violence. We are all inclusive to the point that inclusion does not imply any exclusion, as the stratification, isolation, and apparent division of language, groups, and identifiers are the fundamental delusion that we must overcome if we ever want to heal our meta-crises. We must wake up and realize that we, with all our supposed differences of features and attributes, are really all one large family. We all utterly belong to this one, same universe, so it is time we start realizing this simple truth.

Neodharma invites creatures of all ages, animate and inanimate, for membership that encompasses as much as the universe expands. No matter where you are, who you identify yourself to be or what you think of this movement, you are a part as important as its whole.

As Jiddu Krishnamurti once roared:

So one asks, why don't we see? Why don't we see that one of the causes of war is nationalities? Why don't we see that one may be called a Muslim, and another a Christian—why do we fight over names, over propaganda? Do we see it, or just memorize or think about it? You understand, sir, that your consciousness is the rest of mankind. Mankind, like you and others, goes through every form of difficulty, pain, travail, anxiety, loneliness, depression, sorrow, pleasure—every human being goes through this—every human being all over the world. So our consciousness, our being, is the entire humanity. This is so. How unwilling are we to accept such a simple fact, because we are so accustomed to individuality—I, me first. But if you see that your consciousness is shared by all other human beings living on this marvelous earth, then your whole way of living changes. Argument, persuasion, pressure, propaganda, are all terribly useless because it is you who must see this this for yourself.

Meditation/Prayer App

Neodharma seeks to go beyond the common-sense undestanding of meditation with 'mindfulness' or typical meditation that involves an object of meditation: how may this be so? By subscendence rather than transcendence, seeping deeper into embodiment and finding comfort in what is typically seen as discomfort.

We firmly believe that all the diverse meditation practices we have now have their own merit with some being completely lost to the meditator while other seem to complete them beyond any notion of bliss. Neodharma approach to meditation will always be in the form of this question: "Where do you think you're going?" by reconciling that through a relization that the journey is the destination.
