When I think of the deep dark, conventionally, I see the illuminative technologies that have brightened our nights to the drowning of starlight out of our needs for safety, comfort, alit concrete rivers. More spiritually, I connect this to a poem by William E. Stafford:

If you don't know the kind of person I am
and I don't know the kind of person you are
a pattern that others made may prevail in the world
and following the wrong god home we may miss our star.

For there is many a small betrayal in the mind,
a shrug that lets the fragile sequence break
sending with shouts the horrible errors of childhood
storming out to play through the broken dike.

And as elephants parade holding each elephant's tail,
but if one wanders the circus won't find the park,
I call it cruel and maybe the root of all cruelty
to know what occurs but not recognize the fact.

And so I appeal to a voice, to something shadowy,
a remote important region in all who talk:
though we could fool each other, we should consider—
lest the parade of our mutual life get lost in the dark.

For it is important that awake people be awake,
or a breaking line may discourage them back to sleep;
the signals we give — yes or no, or maybe —
should be clear: the darkness around us is deep.

The star of the poem I want to hone focus on is his metaphoric darkness—a darkness of dimensional depth that we appear to be deep within. As there are levels to darkness, liminal horizontality that borders, encapsulates yet also illuminates in contrast. Think eclipses, dramatic lighting, and lightning itself. That is striking. The many multitudes of darkness also speak to the darknesses of our world. Those shaded areas that underkeep and take in our most hardened emotions.

Likening that to lichen, we can see how lichen breaks down minerals as nutrients to soil. In the same way, can this softening the hardness of intangibly tangible acts of violence—tragic acts of meeting needs to be loved, heard, seen, cared about, and honored wtih living dignity—speak to the nutrients we need break down by breaking down and not lashing out. But to settle in, be the lichen on the rock and truly become rooted in rootlessness and to turn the geological clocks to harboring life in hardened, stone stoic places of resting suffering. Suffering awaiting compassion, not attack or targetting?

Yes, the darkness is ever deep, yet to know the dark, light always creeps.
So in this reverie can we be wander, forage, and fugitively feel?

And so I appeal to a voice, to something shadowy,
a remote important region in all who talk:
though we could fool each other, we should consider—
lest the parade of our mutual life get lost in the dark.

To shower in this muddy, blog, in which its impurities metabolize a deeper truth. That gently approaching what we most want to avoid—those lived out questions—allows us to the dance at the edge of our world, bridging us in bridehood.

To remain in the impervious meeasurement of life in terms of productivity — an off-shoot of industrial free-market capitalism — or to life a life in reminding presence and to be fully there in its blooming vitality.

For the world's love is found in the gravitation of the moon's seas, ever crashing on the banks of the shores of our minds, reminding us of only to those tuning into those secret frequencies kpet away from us in the gamut of life's banks of distractions.
